## Inventory & Supply Operations

This document describes inventory management for an e-commerce platform with real-time stock tracking and a refund lifecycle system.

### Stock Levels & Stock-Out Detection

Every product has a real-time stock count and availability status. Inventory events (restock, return, damage, sale) are timestamped and queryable by type or date range.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Were any top-selling products out of stock yesterday?"
- "Which products are close to stock-out right now?"
- "Show all products with fewer than 20 units remaining."
- "Which items were active in the catalog but had zero stock yesterday?"
- "Did inventory issues reduce conversions yesterday?"
- "Which products were viewed but likely not purchased due to stock issues?"

### Restock Decisions — Human Approval Required

Restocking a product requires human approval before it is committed to prevent over-ordering or restocking discontinued items.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Should we restock any product immediately?" — system recommends, then pauses for approval
- "Restock the top 3 out-of-stock products to 500 units each." — triggers HITL approval
- "Which products need urgent restocking based on yesterday's sell-through rate?"

### Refund Lifecycle

Refunds are tied to specific orders. Each refund has a status: pending, approved, or rejected. The full history is available per user or per product.

Refund creation requires human approval to prevent fraudulent or accidental refunds.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Are refund requests higher than usual this week?"
- "Show all pending refunds from the last 48 hours."
- "Create a refund for order #10045." — triggers HITL approval
- "What is the refund history for customer ID 789?"
- "Which products have the highest refund rate this month?"
- "Show rejected refunds from last quarter."

### Refund Rate Trends

The system computes refund rates over time and flags spikes.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Is the refund rate trending up or down over the past 30 days?"
- "Did the refund rate increase after yesterday's sales drop?"
- "Which product category has the worst refund rate this week?"
- "Show a breakdown of refund rates by category."
- "Did the updated return policy reduce overall refund volume?"

### Inventory Impact on Business

High refund rates (above 15% for a single product) are automatically flagged. Stock-outs on high-demand products are correlated with lost revenue.

Realistic operational questions:
- "How much revenue did we lose yesterday due to stock-outs?"
- "Which out-of-stock products had active campaigns running?"
