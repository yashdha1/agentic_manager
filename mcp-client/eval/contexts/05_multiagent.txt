# Cross-Domain Root Cause Analysis & Corrective Actions (Multi-Agent Eval Context)

This document defines evaluation scenarios requiring **coordinated reasoning across multiple business domains**: sales, inventory, customers, and marketing. No single-domain agent should be sufficient to answer these queries.

---

## Scope & Domains

The system operates across four interacting domains:

* **Sales & Revenue** (orders, revenue, AOV, conversion)
* **Inventory & Supply** (stock levels, stock-outs, restocks)
* **Customer Experience** (complaints, refunds, reviews, sentiment)
* **Marketing & Campaigns** (campaigns, discounts, ROI, channel spend)

All reasoning must integrate signals across these domains.

---

## Cross-Domain Root Cause Diagnosis

The assistant must identify **why a business metric changed**, not just describe what changed.

Typical questions:

* Why did sales drop yesterday?
* Was the drop caused by inventory, marketing, or customer issues?
* Correlate complaint spikes with revenue decline.
* Did stock-outs coincide with campaign activity?
* Did refund spikes contribute to revenue loss?
* Was a regional drop caused by demand, stock, or marketing changes?

Expected behavior:

* Identify primary driver(s)
* Rank contributing factors
* Use evidence across multiple domains
* Avoid single-cause assumptions unless strongly supported

---

## Cross-Domain Correlation Analysis

The system must detect relationships between domains:

* Campaign performance vs inventory availability
* Complaint volume vs sales decline
* Refund rates vs product quality or policy changes
* Discounts vs conversion uplift
* Regional performance vs campaign targeting or stock availability

Typical questions:

* Did campaign drop correlate with stock-outs?
* Which products had both high complaints and declining sales?
* Were high refund products also heavily discounted?
* Did campaigns drive uplift in specific categories?
* Did customer segments change behavior after stock issues?

---

## Historical Memory & Pattern Matching

The system should compare current events with historical patterns in the dataset.

Typical questions:

* Has this type of sales drop happened before?
* What caused similar downturns previously?
* Did discounts work in past recoveries?
* What actions previously improved performance?
* Did stock-outs occur in similar past incidents?

Expected behavior:

* Retrieve similar historical scenarios
* Compare outcomes
* Identify effective past interventions
* Avoid assuming external-world knowledge

---

## Multi-Step Corrective Action Planning (HITL Required)

When issues are diagnosed, the system proposes corrective actions across domains.

### Key Rule

No corrective action is executed automatically.

All actions require human approval.

---

### Example Action Chains

#### 1. Sales Drop Investigation

User: "Fix the issue."

System may propose:

* Restock low-availability products (Inventory)
* Pause campaigns on out-of-stock items (Marketing)
* Trigger discount on underperforming products (Sales)
* Notify affected customers (Customer Support)

Each step must be individually approved.

**Status: Pending Human Approval per step**

---

#### 2. Inventory-Led Issue

User: "Restock affected products."

System:

* Identify impacted SKUs
* Estimate required quantities
* Rank by revenue impact

**Status: Pending Human Approval**

---

#### 3. Marketing Adjustment

User: "Pause worst-performing campaigns."

System:

* Identify underperforming campaigns
* Provide ROI and performance evidence
* Suggest pause or budget shift

**Status: Pending Human Approval**

---

#### 4. Customer Recovery Action

User: "Send recovery message to affected customers."

System:

* Identify impacted customers (failed orders, complaints, refunds)
* Define segmentation
* Propose communication strategy

**Status: Pending Human Approval**

---

## Unified Business Health Analysis

The system must support a consolidated view across all domains:

Typical questions:

* What went wrong yesterday across the business?
* Summarize sales, inventory, marketing, and customer sentiment together.
* Is the business recovering today compared to yesterday?

Expected output:

* Sales impact
* Inventory constraints
* Marketing contribution
* Customer sentiment signals
* Combined root cause explanation

---

## Evaluation Expectations

A correct response must:

* Integrate multiple domains when required
* Avoid single-factor explanations for cross-domain problems
* Correctly identify causal chains (e.g., stock-out → lost sales → complaint increase)
* Use historical comparisons when relevant
* Propose structured corrective actions
* Enforce HITL for all execution steps
* Clearly separate diagnosis vs action planning

---

## Failure Conditions

A response is invalid if it:

* Attributes business changes to only one domain when multiple are relevant
* Ignores inventory/marketing/customer interactions
* Fabricates causality without evidence
* Executes or assumes execution of corrective actions
* Fails to decompose multi-step remediation
