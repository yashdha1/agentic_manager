## Cross-Domain Root Cause Analysis & Corrective Actions

This document describes business scenarios that require coordinated reasoning across sales, inventory, customers, and marketing domains simultaneously.

### Root Cause Diagnosis

The most valuable questions combine signals from multiple domains to find the real cause of a business problem. A single-agent answer is insufficient.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Was the sales drop yesterday caused by inventory issues, marketing failures, or customer problems?"
- "Correlate yesterday's complaint spike with the sales drop."
- "Did out-of-stock items also have active campaigns running — meaning we spent money advertising unavailable products?"
- "Show me all contributing factors for yesterday's revenue drop."
- "Was the drop in a specific region caused by a paused campaign or a stock-out?"
- "Did the refund rate increase because of a product quality issue or a policy change?"

### Cross-Domain Performance Correlation

Realistic operational questions:
- "Did campaign performance drop correlate with the inventory shortage?"
- "Which products had both high complaint volume AND a sales decline yesterday?"
- "Were the products with the highest refund rate the same ones with active discount campaigns?"
- "Did the Black Friday 2024 campaign drive a measurable uplift in Electronics sales?"
- "Compare Platinum customer order frequency before and after the last major stockout."

### Memory-Based Historical Context

The system should surface what happened in similar past situations to inform current decisions.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Has a sales drop like this happened before?"
- "What did we do last time sales dropped significantly?"
- "Did discounts help recover sales in previous similar incidents?"
- "Which corrective actions worked best in past downturns?"
- "What was the outcome last time we ran a flash discount on Electronics?"
- "Did we have stock-out issues the last time this product category underperformed?"

### Corrective Action Sequences — All Require Human Approval

When root cause is identified, the user will ask the system to fix it. The system proposes actions, explains reasoning, then pauses for human approval before executing each step.

Realistic operational questions and the expected action sequence:
- "Fix the issue." → System proposes: restock affected products + pause campaigns on OOS items + send recovery notification to affected customers → each step pauses for HITL approval
- "Restock affected products." → triggers inventory HITL for stock update
- "Run a 10% discount on the top 3 affected products." → triggers sales HITL for product/discount update
- "Pause the worst-performing campaign." → triggers knowledge HITL for campaign status update
- "Send a recovery message to customers who had failed orders yesterday." → triggers customers HITL for newsletter send

### Unified Business Health Check

Realistic operational questions:
- "Give me a full picture of what went wrong yesterday across all domains."
- "Summarise yesterday's business performance: sales, inventory, campaigns, and customer sentiment."
- "Is the business recovering today compared to yesterday across all metrics?"
