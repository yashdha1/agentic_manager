## Sales & Revenue Operations

This document describes sales and revenue data for an e-commerce platform selling consumer electronics, home appliances, sports equipment, and lifestyle products.

### Daily Sales Monitoring

The platform tracks daily revenue, order count, and average order value. Data is available by day, week, and month for comparison.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Why did sales drop yesterday?"
- "Compare yesterday's sales with the same day last week."
- "Is today's revenue tracking above or below yesterday?"
- "Was the drop caused by fewer orders or lower average order value?"
- "Did sales recover today or is the downward trend continuing?"
- "Is this drop a normal fluctuation or a statistical anomaly?"

### Product-Level Sales

Each product has daily and cumulative sales figures, margin data, and a health score combining return rate, rating, and complaint volume.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Which products contributed most to yesterday's revenue drop?"
- "Which product categories are underperforming this week?"
- "Show me the top 5 products by sales margin today."
- "Which products had zero sales yesterday but were active?"
- "Are there products with declining sales over the past 7 days?"

### Regional & Channel Performance

Orders are segmented by region (North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific, Middle East) and channel (web, mobile app, in-store).

Realistic operational questions:
- "Did any region perform worse than usual yesterday?"
- "Which channel had the steepest sales drop?"
- "Compare mobile vs desktop order volume this week."
- "Is the Asia-Pacific region recovering or still declining?"

### Order Analysis

Orders carry: channel, payment method (credit card, UPI, PayPal, COD), payment status (paid, pending, failed), device type, and amount.

Realistic operational questions:
- "How many orders are stuck in pending payment status?"
- "Did failed payments increase yesterday?"
- "Show high-value orders above $500 that were placed but not paid."

### Anomaly Detection

The system can flag statistical anomalies in sales volume, revenue, or order count relative to historical baselines.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Is the sales drop yesterday an anomaly or within normal variance?"
- "Flag any products with an unusual spike or drop in orders."
- "Were there any anomalies in sales across all categories yesterday?"

### Product Updates — Human Approval Required

Changes to product status (active/inactive), stock quantity, order status, or product details pause for human approval before executing.

Realistic operational questions:
- "Restock the top 3 affected products." — triggers HITL approval
- "Mark out-of-stock products as inactive temporarily." — triggers HITL approval
- "Update the order status for stuck pending orders." — triggers HITL approval
